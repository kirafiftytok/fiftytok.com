TEMPLATE 1: CLASSIC PROFESSIONAL COVER LETTER
----------------------------------------------------
Works for: Most applications, corporate jobs, standard format

[Your Name]
[Your Email] | [Your Phone]
[Date]

[Hiring Manager Name]
[Their Title]
[Company Name]
[Company Address]

Dear [Mr./Ms. Last Name or "Hiring Team"],

PARAGRAPH 1: THE HOOK
I'm applying for the [Job Title] position at [Company]. [One sentence 
about why you're excited about this specific role or company — show 
you've done research]. With [X years] of experience in [relevant field], 
I'm confident I can [key value you'll add].

PARAGRAPH 2: YOUR FIT
In my current role at [Company], I [specific achievement relevant to 
job description]. I also [another relevant accomplishment with metrics]. 
What draws me to [Company] is [specific thing about their mission, 
product, culture, or recent news]. I'd bring the same [skill/approach] 
to help [specific goal from job description].

PARAGRAPH 3: THE BRIDGE
Beyond my experience in [core skill], I offer [secondary relevant skill] 
and [another strength]. I'm particularly excited about [specific project, 
team, or challenge mentioned in posting]. I'd love to contribute by 
[specific way you'd add value].

PARAGRAPH 4: THE CLOSE
I'd welcome the chance to discuss how my background in [area] could 
support [Company]'s goals for [specific initiative]. I'm available 
[your availability] and can be reached at [phone/email].

Thank you for considering my application.

Sincerely,
[Your Name]

====================================================
          RESUME WRITING CHEAT SHEET
====================================================

THE GOLDEN RULES
----------------

1. ACHIEVEMENTS > RESPONSIBILITIES
   Bad: "Responsible for managing social media accounts"
   Good: "Grew Instagram from 5K to 50K in 8 months"

2. NUMBERS = CREDIBILITY
   Bad: "Improved sales significantly"
   Good: "Increased quarterly sales by 34% ($240K)"

3. RECENT = RELEVANT
   Last 2-3 roles get the most space
   Older jobs get 1-3 bullets max

4. ONE PAGE (USUALLY)
   New grad to 10 years: 1 page
   Senior/executive: 2 pages okay
   Nobody reads 3+ pages

5. TAILOR EVERY TIME
   Read the job posting
   Use their language
   Address their specific needs


POWER VERBS BY CATEGORY
-----------------------

Leadership: Led, Directed, Managed, Oversaw, Coordinated, Guided
Achievement: Achieved, Exceeded, Delivered, Generated, Increased
Creation: Built, Designed, Developed, Created, Launched, Implemented
Analysis: Analyzed, Evaluated, Assessed, Researched, Identified
Communication: Presented, Negotiated, Collaborated, Advised, Trained


FORMAT BASICS
-------------

• Font: 10-12pt, readable (Arial, Calibri, Georgia)
• Margins: 0.5" to 1"
• Sections: Clear headers, consistent formatting
• File: PDF unless they ask for Word
• Filename: "FirstName_LastName_Resume.pdf"


THE 6-SECOND TEST
-----------------

Recruiters spend 6 seconds on first scan. They look at:
1. Current/recent title
2. Current company
3. Previous company
4. Education
5. Dates/tenure
6. Keywords

Make these pop visually.


COMMON MISTAKES
---------------

✗ Typos (have someone proofread)
✗ Generic objective statements
✗ Including references on resume
✗ Unrelated hobbies
✗ Photos (in US)
✗ Personal info like age, SSN
✗ Fancy graphics that break ATS
✗ Lying


====================================================
         COVER LETTER WRITING TIPS
====================================================

STRUCTURE THAT WORKS
--------------------

1. Opening Hook (1-2 sentences)
   - Name the role
   - Why THIS company (specific!)
   - Quick value statement

2. Evidence Paragraph (3-4 sentences)
   - Your best relevant achievement
   - Connect to what they need
   - Use numbers if possible

3. Bridge Paragraph (2-3 sentences)
   - Additional relevant skills
   - Something specific about the role
   - Show you read the posting

4. Close (2-3 sentences)
   - Clear call to action
   - Contact info
   - Thank them


PRO TIPS
--------

• Mirror their language. They say "fast-paced," you say "fast-paced"
• Do real research. Mention something only interested candidates know
• Show, don't tell. "I'm detail-oriented" < "I caught a $50K billing error"
• Keep it to one page. 3-4 paragraphs max
• Different letter for each application


WHAT NOT TO WRITE
-----------------

✗ "To Whom It May Concern"
✗ "I am writing to apply for..." (obvious)
✗ Restating your entire resume
✗ "I think I would be a great fit"
✗ Salary expectations (unless asked)
✗ Why you need the job
✗ Negativity about past employers
