TEMPLATE 2: CLIENT INTAKE QUESTIONNAIRE
============================================

[Your Business Name] — Client Intake Questionnaire

Please complete this form before our kickoff call. Takes about 10-15 minutes.

ABOUT YOU & YOUR BUSINESS
1. What's your business name and what do you do in one sentence?
2. Who is your ideal customer? (Be specific — age, job, challenges, goals)
3. What makes you different from competitors?
4. What's your website/social links?

ABOUT THIS PROJECT
5. What are we creating together? (In your own words)
6. What does success look like for this project?
7. What's the #1 thing you want people to feel/do when they see this?
8. Any examples of work you love? (Links or descriptions)
9. Any specific no-gos or things you hate?

LOGISTICS
10. What's your target deadline? (Hard deadline vs. preferred)
11. Who's the final decision-maker on this project?
12. Who else needs to approve deliverables?
13. How do you prefer to communicate? (Email, Slack, calls, async video)
14. How often do you want updates?

BUDGET & EXPECTATIONS
15. Have you worked with a freelancer/agency/consultant before?
16. What would make this project feel like a total win?
17. What would make it feel like a failure?
18. Anything else I should know?

TEMPLATE 3: KICKOFF CALL AGENDA
============================================

Project Kickoff Call — [Client Name]
Date: [Date]
Duration: 45-60 minutes

BEFORE THE CALL
[ ] Review client intake questionnaire
[ ] Prepare 2-3 initial ideas/approaches
[ ] Have contract and payment info ready

AGENDA

1. Introductions & Vibe Check (5 min)
- Quick intros if first time meeting
- How's their week going? (Build rapport)

2. Confirm Project Scope (10 min)
- What we're creating
- What's included / not included
- Deliverable format

3. Goals & Success Metrics (10 min)
- What does success look like?
- How will we measure it?
- Primary vs. secondary goals

4. Timeline & Milestones (10 min)
- Key dates and deadlines
- Review/feedback touchpoints
- What could cause delays? (Get ahead of blockers)

5. Communication & Process (5 min)
- How we'll communicate
- Feedback process
- What they can expect from me

6. Questions & Next Steps (10 min)
- Client questions
- Confirm next action items
- Schedule follow-up if needed

AFTER THE CALL
[ ] Send meeting notes within 24 hours
[ ] Send project brief for approval
[ ] Begin work per agreed timeline

TEMPLATE 7: SCOPE CHANGE REQUEST
============================================

Subject: Quick check-in: Change to [Project] scope

Hey [Client Name],

I wanted to flag a scope change before moving forward:

THE REQUEST:
[Describe what the client asked for that's outside original scope]

WHY IT'S A CHANGE:
Our original agreement included [X], but this request would involve [Y], which is additional work.

OPTIONS:

1. Add it to this project 
   - Additional investment: $[Amount]
   - Impact on timeline: [None / Adds X days]

2. Save it for Phase 2
   - We finish current scope on time/budget
   - Tackle this in a follow-up project

3. Swap priorities
   - We can replace [existing deliverable] with this new request
   - No additional cost, but you'd lose [what's being removed]

Just let me know which option works for you, and I'll update the project brief accordingly.

No wrong answer here — just want to make sure we're aligned!

[Your Name]

BONUS: CLIENT RED FLAGS CHECKLIST
============================================

Watch out for these during onboarding:

COMMUNICATION RED FLAGS
- Doesn't respond for days, then expects immediate replies from you
- Wants to discuss everything on the phone, nothing in writing
- Vague answers to direct questions
- "You're the expert, just figure it out" (sounds nice, leads to endless revisions)

SCOPE RED FLAGS
- "We'll figure out the details as we go"
- "This should be quick and easy"
- "I don't know exactly what I want, but I'll know it when I see it"
- Compares your pricing to Fiverr or their nephew

BUDGET RED FLAGS
- Pushes back on deposit requirement
- "Let's do a small test project" (at heavily discounted rate)
- Tries to negotiate after agreeing
- Mentions they've "been burned by freelancers before" multiple times

DECISION-MAKING RED FLAGS
- Multiple stakeholders with vague authority
- "I'll need to run this by my partner/board/committee"
- Changes direction after approving something

Not dealbreakers individually, but 3+ = proceed with caution

BONUS: ONBOARDING TIMELINE CHEAT SHEET
============================================

Day 1: Contract signed, payment received
Day 1-2: Send welcome email
Day 2-3: Send client intake questionnaire
Day 3-5: Client returns questionnaire
Day 5-7: Schedule and hold kickoff call
Day 7-10: Send project brief for approval
Day 10+: Begin project work

PRO TIPS:
- Don't start work before deposit clears
- Set deadline for intake questionnaire (or kickoff gets pushed)
- Always get scope approval in writing before starting
- Early professionalism sets expectations for the entire project
