────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
BONUS 1: INVOICE NUMBERING SYSTEM CHEAT SHEET
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

SIMPLE SEQUENTIAL:
INV-001, INV-002, INV-003...
Best for: Low volume, single client type

DATE-BASED:
INV-2024-001, INV-2024-002...
Best for: Tax organization, multiple years

CLIENT-CODED:
ABC-001, XYZ-001 (client initials + number)
Best for: Multiple ongoing clients

PROJECT-CODED:
PROJ1-INV-01, PROJ2-INV-01...
Best for: Large projects with multiple invoices

FULL FORMAT:
[YEAR]-[CLIENT]-[PROJECT]-[SEQ]
Example: 2024-ACME-WEB-003
Best for: Complex businesses, detailed tracking

TIPS:
• Never reuse invoice numbers
• Keep a simple spreadsheet to track
• Start at 001, not 1 (easier sorting)
• Include year for tax purposes
• Consider what your accountant needs


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
BONUS 2: CLIENT RED FLAGS CHECKLIST
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

BEFORE SIGNING:
□ Won't sign a contract
□ Pushes back on reasonable deposit
□ Vague about scope ("you'll figure it out")
□ Previous freelancer "disappeared"
□ Wants unlimited revisions
□ Compares you unfavorably to cheaper options
□ Rush project but slow to answer questions
□ Can't clearly explain what they want
□ "This will lead to more work" as payment

DURING PROJECT:
□ Scope creep without discussion
□ Slow to provide feedback, then demands rush
□ Goes dark, then emerges with urgency
□ Changes mind frequently after approval
□ Involves new stakeholders mid-project
□ Questions every line item
□ "Quick" requests that aren't quick

PAYMENT RED FLAGS:
□ Asks to delay invoice
□ Disputes charges after work delivered
□ Partial payments without agreement
□ Changes payment method last minute
□ Claims budget was different than discussed

TRUST YOUR GUT. A bad client costs more than no client.


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
BONUS 3: CONTRACT TERMS GLOSSARY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY (IP)
Who owns the work. Usually transfers to client upon full payment.

WORK FOR HIRE
Client owns work from the moment it's created. Stronger than IP 
transfer. Common in US employment, less common for freelancers.

KILL FEE
Payment owed if client cancels project early. Protects your time.

NET 30 / NET 14
Payment due within 30/14 days of invoice date.

SCOPE OF WORK (SOW)
Specific description of what you will deliver. The more detailed,
the better.

SCOPE CREEP
When project expands beyond original agreement. Contract should
address how to handle this.

DELIVERABLES
The actual things you're handing over (files, designs, etc.).

REVISION ROUNDS
How many times client can request changes. Define this clearly.

INDEMNIFICATION
Protection from legal claims. Be careful with broad indemnity clauses.

LIMITATION OF LIABILITY
Caps your financial exposure. Usually limited to fees paid.

FORCE MAJEURE
"Acts of God" - excuses performance during extraordinary circumstances.

NON-COMPETE
Restricts working with competitors. Negotiate carefully or refuse.

NON-SOLICITATION
Can't hire client's employees or steal their clients.

CONFIDENTIALITY / NDA
Keeps project information private.

GOVERNING LAW
Which state/country's laws apply to disputes.

TIP: When in doubt about contract terms, consult a lawyer.
These templates are starting points, not legal advice.
