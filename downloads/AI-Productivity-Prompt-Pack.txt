CATEGORY 1: EMAIL & COMMUNICATION
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

1.1 PROFESSIONAL EMAIL COMPOSER
--------------------------------
I need to write a professional email about [TOPIC].

Context:
- Recipient: [NAME/ROLE]
- Relationship: [formal/casual/first contact]
- Goal: [what I want to achieve]
- Key points to include: [list them]
- Tone: [professional/friendly/urgent]

Write a clear, concise email that gets to the point quickly. Keep it under 150 words unless complexity requires more.


1.2 MEETING FOLLOW-UP GENERATOR
--------------------------------
Write a follow-up email after a meeting.

Meeting details:
- Topic: [TOPIC]
- Attendees: [NAMES]
- Key decisions made: [list]
- Action items: [list with owners]
- Next steps: [what happens next]

Keep it scannable with bullet points. Include clear deadlines where mentioned.


1.3 DIFFICULT CONVERSATION FRAMER
----------------------------------
Help me frame a difficult message about [SITUATION].

The challenge: [what makes this hard]
My goal: [desired outcome]
Their likely concerns: [what they might worry about]
Relationship context: [boss/peer/report/client]

Write a message that:
- Leads with empathy
- States the issue clearly
- Proposes a path forward
- Maintains the relationship


1.4 APOLOGY/EXPLANATION EMAIL
------------------------------
I need to apologize for [SITUATION].

What happened: [brief description]
Impact: [how it affected them]
The reason (not excuse): [context if relevant]
What I'm doing to fix it: [concrete actions]

Write a genuine apology that takes responsibility without over-apologizing. Keep it dignified.


1.5 REQUEST/PROPOSAL EMAIL
---------------------------
I'm requesting [WHAT] from [WHO].

Context: [why I need this]
Benefit to them: [what's in it for them]
Specifics: [exact ask, timeline, requirements]
Backup option: [alternative if they can't do full ask]

Write a persuasive but not pushy request. Make it easy for them to say yes.


1.6 STATUS UPDATE EMAIL
------------------------
Write a status update email for [PROJECT/INITIATIVE].

Current status: [on track/delayed/ahead/blocked]
Key accomplishments since last update: [list]
Upcoming milestones: [what's next]
Blockers or risks: [if any]
Help needed: [if any]

Format for quick scanning. Lead with the most important info.


1.7 REJECTION LETTER (KIND VERSION)
------------------------------------
I need to decline [REQUEST/APPLICATION/INVITATION].

What I'm declining: [specifics]
Real reason (for my context): [why]
What I can share: [appropriate explanation]
Relationship to preserve: [context]
Alternative to offer: [if any]

Write a kind but clear rejection. Don't leave false hope or be vague.


1.8 INTRODUCTION/NETWORKING EMAIL
----------------------------------
I want to introduce myself to [PERSON] for [PURPOSE].

Who they are: [role, context]
How I found them: [referral/their work/event]
What I admire/noticed: [genuine connection point]
What I'm hoping for: [specific, reasonable ask]
What I offer: [value I bring]

Write a brief, memorable intro email. No more than 100 words. Make it easy to respond.
