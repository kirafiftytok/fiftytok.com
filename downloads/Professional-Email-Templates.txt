HOW TO USE THESE TEMPLATES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

1. Find the template that fits your situation
2. Copy it into your email client
3. Replace [bracketed text] with your specifics
4. Adjust tone to match your relationship with recipient
5. Proofread and send!

Pro tip: Save your favorites as email drafts for quick access.

TEMPLATE 1: COLD OUTREACH EMAIL
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Subject: Quick question about [specific topic/their work]

I came across [specific thing you noticed — article, project, 
post] and wanted to reach out.

[One sentence about why it caught your attention]

I'm curious about [specific question related to their work].

Would you be open to a quick 15-minute call sometime this week? 
Happy to work around your schedule.

Thanks,
[Your name]

        BONUS: EMAIL BEST PRACTICES CHEAT SHEET
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

SUBJECT LINES
- Be specific: "Q2 Budget Review — Need Approval by Friday"
- Not vague: "Quick question" or "Checking in"

STRUCTURE
- Lead with the ask or key point
- Use formatting (bullets, bold) for scannability
- End with clear next steps

TONE
- Match the recipient's style
- Be direct but not blunt
- When in doubt, be warmer than you think necessary

TIMING
- Schedule for business hours
- Don't expect instant replies
- Follow up once, then let it go

PROOFREADING
- Read it out loud
- Check names and attachments
- Sleep on important emails
