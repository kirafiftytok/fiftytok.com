PART 6: SYSTEM SETUP GUIDE
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

STEP 1: Choose Your Tool
─────────────────────────
Any tool works. Pick based on your needs:
• Notion: Visual thinkers, databases, collaboration
• Obsidian: Linking, plain text, privacy
• Roam: Daily notes, networked thought
• Apple Notes: Simplicity, iOS integration
• Google Docs: Zero learning curve

Don't overthink this. You can migrate later.

STEP 2: Create Your Structure
─────────────────────────────
Minimum viable structure:

📥 Inbox (everything goes here first)
📁 Projects (active work with deadlines)
📁 Areas (ongoing responsibilities)
📁 Resources (reference material by topic)
📁 Archive (completed/inactive stuff)
📅 Daily Notes (optional but recommended)

STEP 3: Build the Habit
───────────────────────
Daily (5-10 min):
• Capture anything interesting into Inbox
• Process yesterday's captures
• Write daily note (optional)

Weekly (30 min):
• Full inbox clear
• Review active projects
• Weekly reflection

Monthly (1 hour):
• Archive completed projects
• Review and update areas
• Prune resources

STEP 4: Trust the Process
─────────────────────────
Your second brain will feel empty at first. That's normal. 
The value compounds over time.

After 30 days: You'll start finding old notes useful
After 90 days: You'll wonder how you lived without it
After 1 year: Your past self becomes your best collaborator

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
BONUS: Common Mistakes to Avoid
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

1. Over-organizing early — Start messy, organize later
2. Saving everything — If you won't use it, don't keep it
3. Perfect formatting — Searchable beats pretty
4. Too many folders — Flat > deep hierarchy
5. Forgetting to review — A system you don't use dies
6. Switching tools constantly — Pick one and commit for 90 days
